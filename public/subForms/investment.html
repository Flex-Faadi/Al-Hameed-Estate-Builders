Karachi – the economic engine of Pakistan – boasts an incredibly significant position in the real estate sector of the country. The city is mainly known for its diversity and for featuring a delightful mix of high-end, middle, and low-income neighbourhoods.

The developing real estate product of the city primarily consists of high-rise apartments, but the city isn’t short of options when it comes to finding a vacant piece of land or a house.

Scheme 33

Sitting on the edges of Karachi-Hyderabad Motorway, Scheme 33 maintains the top position when it comes to buying properties in the city of lights. This popularity is mainly due to the affordable investment options and planned communities that are made available to Karachiites.

Proposed as a regulated housing program in 1971, Scheme 33, Karachi spans over an area of 26,026 acres, and it is expected to execute around 137 housing schemes. Out of those 137 housing schemes, a few have already been executed, developed, and populated with houses and apartments.

For further guidance on whether you should consider investing in Scheme 33, Karachi, or somewhere else, please navigate to our recently published analysis where we have listed the societies marked safe for investment and discussed their prices as well.

Gulistan-e-Johar

hose looking for investment opportunities in Karachi’s real estate market also showed interest in investing in Gulistan-e-Jauhar. It doesn’t come as something unexpected since this housing scheme has always raked highly among the top searched areas for investment in Karachi.

Expanding near the Jinnah International Airport, Gulistan-e-Jauhar is mainly known for featuring affordable housing units and investment opportunities, along with standard infrastructure and amenities ensuring a reasonable lifestyle. The abundant supply of residential utilities such as electricity, water, and gas has made this project one of the most reliable developments in the city.
In addition to basic residential facilities, Gulistan-e-Jauhar is also home to an abundance of educational institutions, recreational facilities, sports complexes, parks, and commercial markets.

North Karachi

North Karachi Township is located on the northern edges of Karachi, past North Nazimabad. The town comprises six sectors with a mix of residential and commercial real estate investment opportunities. Let’s discuss the possibilities of buying a property in North Karachi in detail using the data we’ve gathered

Surrounded by prominent areas like Buffer Zone, New Karachi Town, Surjani Town, Naya Nazimabad, Gulshan-e-Maymar, and North Nazimabad, North Karachi caters primarily to the low and middle-income families of Karachi. They took up residence in the now densely packed society when it was mostly barren lands. It is also known as North Karachi Township, and has 6 sectors, from Sector 7 up to Sector 12.

While North Karachi features both houses and flats, most apartment buildings have a commercial storefront on the ground floor, with low-rise or mid-rise residential structures catering to both buyers and renters. Prominent routes within the society include Shahrah-e-Usman and Shahrah-e-Noor Jahan, which pass through the development and are also its commercial hubs. Chowrangis and Chowks, including Anda Mor, Disco Mor, Nagan Chowrangi, Power House Chowrangi, and Saba Cinema Roundabout, play a major role in the area for the locals, as these are the main places for trade and commerce with weekly area markets providing all the essentials at affordable rates.

Other prominent landmarks in or near the society include Hira Park, North Karachi Graveyard, Iqra University’s (IU) North campus, North Karachi Gym, Club, & Swimming Pool, Tikon Park, Rashid Latif Cricket Academy, and Aiwan-e-Tijarat-o-Sanat Hospital, along with campuses of several reputable schools and colleges. Sakhi Hassan Graveyard, Manghopir Lake, Naya Nazimabad Cricket Ground, Sohrab Goth, North Karachi’s Industrial Area, the University of Karachi (UoK), Manghopir Road, and the M-9 Karachi-Hyderabad Motorway are some of the other prominent landmarks near this residential community.

Surjani Town

Surjani Town in District West is being pitched as a new avenue for investors or low- and middle-income people who want to own a house.

Surjani Town is one of the neighborhoods of Gadap Town, and if you look at the map, it is to the left of Karachi spread over 6,600 acres of land that belongs to the Karachi Development Authority or KDA. Surjani Town has 17 sectors in which 15 have been set aside for housing schemes that were launched by the KDA in 1984.
Real estate agents who work in the area find that they are mostly dealing with working class clients who want to live in their own house and will buy plots that are 60, 80, and 120 square yards.

“People mostly prefer 120 square yard plots for residential use so that is why prices have gone a bit over a year in different sectors,” he said, naming Sectors L-2, L-4, L-5, L-6, L-7 and L-10. L-2, 5, 6, 7 have price tags of Rs3 million to Rs3.5 million.

Some of these goths, however, were part of Karachi’s original list and have been documented by Perween Rehman. The mapped goths include Brohi Goth, Saiful Marri Goth, Yousuf Goth, Dil Murad Khan Goth, and Ayub Goth.

Defence-Phase-VII

Properties for sale in DHA Defence, Karachi, have retained their position as one of the most popular and safe options to secure your investment in Pakistan’s property market. It is no secret that the housing projects executed by the Defence Housing Authority are considered among the most secure residential areas across the country. Owing to the vigilant laws against expansion and encroachment, the housing schemes in this area come with a by-default guarantee of appreciation.

Karachi’s Defence is mainly known for its near-perfect location where major arteries of the city are stretched across the area, making it accessible from every corner of the city. It is situated in close proximity to Clifton and Sea View. DHA Defence comprises several phases and each phase is known for its unique offerings.
Schools, hospitals, religious centres, malls, commercial markets, parks, and other such facilities that elevate the standards of life are scattered everywhere in the community. Each phase of this area is connected with the other, making it extremely easy for residents to navigate and avail the facilities of nearby phases.
